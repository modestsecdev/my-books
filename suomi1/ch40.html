Jane tutustuu Lehtisen perheeseen / Jane gets acquainted with the Lehtinen family#

Suomi

English

1. Ritva L. (on the phone). Yes, Jane, could you come and have afternoon coffee with us at four on Sunday? We could get to know each other better, and you’re interested to see a Finnish home, aren’t you?

2. J. Kiitos kutsusta, tulen oikein mielellani.

2. J. Thank you for the invitation, I’ll be very glad to come.

3. R. Nakemiin sitten sunnuntaina ja tervetuloa!

4. R. Hello, Jane, nice to have you here!

5. J. Hello! I’ve got some flowers for you here.

6. R. Ahaa, sina tunnet jo suomalaisia tapoja. Kiitos kauniista kukista!

6. R. Ah, you’re familiar with some Finnish customs, already. Thank you for the beautiful flowers!

7. J. Mina toin myos pienen lahjan lapsille, vahan suklaata. Sinullahan on suuri perhe.

7. J. I also brought a little present for the children, a little chocolate. You have a large family, I understand.

8. R. Niin, meita on kahdeksan, kolme aikuista ja viisi lasta. Minun aitini asuu meilla. Kiitos vain, lapsista on aina kiva saada suklaata.

11. Arto. Ritva on kertonut, etta sina olet lehtinainen. Mihin lehteen sina kirjoitat?

13. Isoaiti. Minkalaisista aiheista?

13. Grandmother. About what sort of topics?

14. J. Melkein mista vain. Eri maiden ihmisista, heidan ongelmistaan, tavoistaan ja kodeistaan. Mina kirjoitan paljon naisista ja lapsista, vahemman miehista.

14. J. Almost anything. About people in different countries, their problems, customs, and homes. I write a lot about women and children, less about men.

15. Arto. Oletko sina feministi?

15. Arto. Are you a feminist?

16. J. Not a radical feminist, but I really am very much interested in the position of women in different countries.

17. R. Otetaampas nyt kahvia. Ottakaa kahvileipaa, se on kotona leivottua. Muuten, Jane, onko sinusta jo tullut hyva hiihtaja?

17. R. Well, let’s have some coffee now. Take some cakes, they’re home baked. By the way, Jane, have you become a good skier already?

18. J. Ei ihan viela. Kun aloittaa aikuisena, oppii luonnollisesti paljon hitaammin. Mina pelkaan aina kaatumista. Mutta hauskaa se on.

18. J. Not quite yet. When you start as a grown-up, you learn much more slowly, of course. I’m always afraid of falling. But it’s fun.

19. Arto. Olitko sina hiihtanyt koskaan, ennen kuin tulit Suomeen?

19. Arto. Had you ever done any skiing before you came to Finland?

20. J. Olin mina kerran yrittanyt slalomia. Mutta se oli ihan mahdotonta. Minun mielestani tama suomalainen hiihto sopii minulle paremmin.

20. J. Yes, I had once tried slalom. But that was quite impossible. I think the Finnish way of skiing suits me better.

21. Isoaiti. Muuten, missa te tutustuitte toisiinne?

21. Grandmother. By the way, where did you meet each other?

22. R. Jane ja minako? Me olimme samoilla kutsuilla. Minulla oli ollut siella hirvean ikavaa, mutta sitten mina tapasin Janen. Meista tuli heti ystavat.

22. R. Jane and me? We were at the same party. I had been terribly bored there, but then I met Jane. We became friends right away.

23. J. Minusta tuntui, etta me olimme tunteneet toisemme jo kauan aikaa. Ja minusta on todella hauska tutustua myos Ritvan perheeseen.

23. J. I had the feeling that we had known each other for a long time. And it’s really a pleasure for me to meet Ritva’s family, too.

24. R. Voi, mina unohdan tarjota lisaa kahvia. Saako olla toinen kuppi?

25. J. Kylla kiitos, sinun kahvisi on erinomaista. Samoin tama mustikkapiirakka. Se ihan sulaa suussa.

25. J. Yes, please, your coffee is excellent. So is this blueberry pie. It really melts in the mouth.

26. R. Ota sitten lisaa piirakkaa! Arto, sina kai otat mieluummin pullaa?

26. R. Then do have some more pie, please! Arto, you probably prefer pulla?

27. Arto. Niin otan. Mina pidan enemman pullasta kuin makeista kahvileivista.

27. Arto. Yes, I do. I prefer pulla with coffee to sweet things.

28. R. Oletteko te kaikki maistaneet tata kakkua? Se ei ole liian makeaa. Ole hyva, aiti. Saako olla kolmas kuppi?

28. R. Have you tasted this cake, all of you? It isn’t too sweet. Have some, mother. What about a third cup?

2. “He pitavat toisistaan” (each other)#

(Mina rakastan sinua
ja sina rakastat minua.)

Me rakastamme toisia/mme.      We love each other.
Te rakastatte toisia/nne.      You love each other.
He rakastavat toisia/an (-nsa). They love each other.

As a reciprocal pronoun (“each other”) Finnish uses the pl. forms of toinen provided with a poss. suffix.

Further examples:

Finnish

English

Me tunnemme toise/mme (toiset + mme)

We know each other.

He keskustelevat usein toiste/nsa kanssa (toisten + nsa)

They often talk to each other.

He pitavat toisista/an.

They are fond of each other.

Missa te tutustuitte toisii/nne (toisiin + nne)

Where did you meet each other?

Kirjoitimme ennen toisille/mme.

We used to write to each other.

3. Pluperfect tense#

nah/da to see

Affirmative                  Negative
olin       nahnyt   I had seen      en     ollut nahnyt   I had not seen
olit       nahnyt   you had seen    et     ollut nahnyt   you had not seen
oli        nahnyt   he/she had seen ei     ollut nahnyt   he/she had not seen
olimme     nahneet  we had seen     emme   olleet nahneet we had not seen
olitte     nahneet  you had seen    ette   olleet nahneet you had not seen
olivat     nahneet  they had seen   eivat  olleet nahneet they had not seen

Question:
Olitko (sina) nahnyt? etc.   had you seen? etc.

Negative question:
etko (sina) ollut nahnyt? etc. hadn't you seen? etc.

Structure:

  • The auxiliary olla is inflected in the past tense;

  • The main verb is in the past participle (lesson 31:1): sing. nah/nyt, pl. nah/neet.
