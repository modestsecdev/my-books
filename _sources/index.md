## Physics — J.J. Thomson

### [Electricity and Magnetism](../elctricity-magnetism/ch1)

A comprehensive treatment of classical electromagnetic theory, covering electrostatics, electric currents, magnetism, and electromagnetic induction. Written as a university-level textbook and drawing on the foundations laid by Maxwell and Faraday.

### [Electricity and Matter](../Electricity-matter/ch1)

Six lectures delivered at Yale University in 1903. Thomson outlines his views on the nature of electricity, the structure of matter, and the evidence for the electron — one of his greatest discoveries.

### [Recollections and Reflections](../Recollections-And-Reflections/ch1)

Thomson's autobiography, written in his later years. A vivid account of Victorian-era science education, life at the Cavendish Laboratory in Cambridge, and the remarkable generation of physicists who shaped the modern world.

## Physics — James Clerk Maxwell

### [An Elementary Treatise on Electricity](../elementary-treatise-electricity-Garnett/README)

Maxwell's accessible introduction to electricity, edited by William Garnett and published posthumously in 1881. Covers electrostatics, electric currents, and the foundations of electromagnetic theory with characteristic clarity.
